E-junkie: Review - the good the bad and the ugly

For those that don't know, E-Junkie.com is a versatile platform to sell e-goods or physical products. E-Goods are in essence downloadable things a customer can pay then download from E-Junkie.

The setup

In essence you sign up, add your products and then setup your website or social media to point to E-Junkie. You can also create a shop on their website if you have a few items and some images and things to upload to them.

  1. Sign up for an account.
  2. Set up your profile, including your PayPal email address, Stripe, etc.
  3. Add the products.
  4. Get the codes, links, or other ways you'll drive your customers to the check out.

They give you a ton of options too.

All in all, the setup was pretty easy and straightforward.

They'll give you a...

  • Secure Digital Delivery so your customers can download the content after purchasing.
  • Shipping calculator so your customers can calculate shipping at checkout.
  • Affiliate programs so third parties can resell your content.
  • Buy Now, Add to Cart, Manage Cart, etc. buttons for your customers to easily check out.
  • Flexible pricing models so customers can pay a fixed price, pay what you want, or donate to you and your organization.
  • Payment integration so your customers can pay using any number of options.
  • PDF protection where they'll stamp each buyer's details in the PDF so you'll know who's sharing your content.
  • SEO-friendly landing pages with Google/Facebook analytics integration
  • Unique download codes for selling while offline.
  • You can create promotions and coupons, and even gift cards.
  • Inventory management so customers can't purchase items you no longer have in stock.
  • List your products in the E-junkie marketplace
  • Track customers using Google/Facebook analytics
  • Personalize the emails customers receive after purchasing your products.
  • Automatically calculate sales tax.

Pricing

Their pricing is very competitive with a flat monthly fee and no transaction fees. Although the customer will see additional fees on checkout depending on their payment system.

The positive

I love the ease of use of the product and the no transaction fees. It keeps the cost low. Their customer support is great too.

The negative

Their website feels outdated. I'm not sure how many updates the app is still getting. Although, not making changes means less errors, bugs and security vulnerabilities so it might not be a bad thing.

Overall Ratings

  • Trustpilot: E-junkie has a rating of 4.0 out of 5 stars based on 23 reviews.
  • G2: The platform has a rating of 3.9 out of 5 stars from 6 reviews.
  • SaaSworthy: E-junkie is rated 4 out of 5 stars based on 29 ratings.

Summary

In summary, I loved E-Junkie. If I ever sell something, it's probably what I'll use. I'm shocked they only have 4.0 stars on third-party rating systems.
